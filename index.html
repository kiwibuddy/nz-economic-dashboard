The indicators — the few numbers that actually matter. Each refreshes daily.

Green/amber/red dots show whether each number is calm, worth watching, or a real drag. The bars show how far an index has fallen from its highest point in the past year (its “drawdown”) — that’s the honest measure of damage done, not the scary one-day headline.

The ground underneath — the video's real thesis: how fragile is the setup? (slow-moving)

These don’t move day to day — they update monthly (margin debt) or quarterly (AI spending). They’re why the video argues a fall would be amplified if it comes: record borrowed money and a circular AI-spending loop. Important: high leverage tells you a drop would be worse, not when it starts. It’s a smoke-detector, not a clock.

A link glows when today’s data suggests that domino is actually wobbling. Most days the early ones move and the later ones don’t — that’s the whole point of watching: you see it coming down the chain, not after it arrives.

What the doom videos get right — and oversell

✓ Real and verified

  • Korea’s KOSPI genuinely crashed ~9–10% in a day and is down roughly a quarter from its June peak.
  • It was a leverage bubble: record margin borrowing, then forced selling of ~320,000+ retail accounts.
  • Two stocks — Samsung & SK Hynix — are more than half the whole index. Real, dangerous concentration.
  • Your KiwiSaver is exposed to the same US AI-tech names.

✕ Overstated / not yet true

  • “It’s already a global crash.” The US is down low single digits; the fear gauge (VIX) is calm.
  • Korea’s scary numbers (-25%, 320k wiped out) get re-used to imply a worldwide collapse. That’s a switch.
  • “This will trigger 2008 again.” A forecast dressed as a fact. 2008 was a banking crisis; this is an equity-leverage unwind.
Full claim-by-claim fact-check with sources is in FACT-CHECK.md alongside this page. Short version: real, serious, still contained. A genuine “watch,” not yet a “brace.”
